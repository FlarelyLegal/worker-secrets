Secret Vault

Your secrets. Your keys. Your infrastructure.

A self-hosted secret manager on Cloudflare Workers where you control every layer. Store secrets only you can decrypt, share them with your team through RBAC, and revoke access without touching the server.

# Deploy the vault to your Cloudflare account
$ hfs deploy

# Generate your encryption identity
$ hfs keygen --register
public key: age196nua3eewwvud6k858la...

# Store a secret only you can read
$ hfs set DB_PASSWORD "hunter2" --private
Stored DB_PASSWORD (e2e private)

# Or share with your team through RBAC
$ hfs set SHARED_KEY "value" --e2e -t production
encrypting for 3 recipients
Stored SHARED_KEY (e2e)

# Someone leaves. Revoke and re-encrypt.
$ hfs user rm alice@co.com
$ hfs rewrap --all
12 secret(s) rewrapped
End-to-end Secrets encrypted on your machine with age before they reach the server. Private or team-shared. The server never sees plaintext.
Envelope Each secret gets its own data encryption key, wrapped by a master key. AES-256-GCM. Key rotation without re-encrypting data.
Integrity HMAC-SHA256 binds every secret to its key name, ciphertext, and encryption keys. Tamper-evident at rest.
Access Cloudflare Access at the edge, JWT validation in the Worker, registered service tokens, RBAC with tag-level restrictions.
Audit Every operation logged with identity, IP, and request ID. SHA-256 hash-chained so modifications are detectable.
Operations Version history, expiry enforcement, burn-after-reading, geo-fencing, webhooks, auto-provisioning. 23 runtime flags, no redeploy.
When you store a secret with --private, it is encrypted with your age key on your machine, then envelope-encrypted with a per-secret key on the server, then integrity-bound with HMAC. Three layers. The server holds ciphertext inside ciphertext. Only your identity file can reverse it.

When you store with --e2e, the CLI asks the server who has access based on roles and tags, encrypts for all of them, and sends the result. Revoke a user, rewrap, and their key is simply not included.
